Similarity estimates combine genetic relationship (language family trees), lexical overlap (shared vocabulary including loanwords from colonial contact, trade, religion), grammatical typology (word order, morphology, case systems), and areal/Sprachbund effects (Balkan convergence, Standard Average European, Sino-Xenic vocabulary sphere). Distances calibrated against FSI difficulty ratings, Ethnologue lexical similarity data, and WALS typological features. Writing system difficulty only factors in for systems requiring significant memorization effort (CJK characters, Arabic abjad); Cyrillic, Hangul, and Greek alphabets are treated as trivially learnable.
